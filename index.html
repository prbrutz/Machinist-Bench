What’s on the bench

Everything you reach for, in one place

The lookups and little calculations a working shop does a dozen times a day — gathered into one fast, offline reference.

Speeds & feeds

Dial in RPM, surface speed, feed, and chip load for turning and milling. Pick the material and cutter; get numbers you can set.

Thread concordance

Pitch, major and minor diameters, and tap drills across roughly 196 threads — from current standards to the long-obsolete.

End-mill math

Ball-nose stepover and scallop height, plus chamfer and dovetail geometry — with diagrams that match the actual cut.

GD&T reference

All 14 geometric characteristics, each with its symbol and a plain-language note on what it actually controls on the part.

Insert decoder

Type an ANSI or ISO insert designation and read it back, character by character — then choose the right insert for the job.

Mark finder

Search around 120 maker’s marks and tooling stamps to put a name to the steel in your hand.

Tool chooser

Answer five questions and get one clear recommendation — the same answer every time, not a guess.

Works offline

Dovetail · over rods

Measure over rods

It draws the joint, then does the trig

Dovetails are easy to cut and hard to measure. Drop two gauge pins into the corners, mic across the outside, and read the width straight off — for a male tongue or a female socket. The bench draws the geometry and works the rod math for you, and the reading doesn’t even depend on depth.
